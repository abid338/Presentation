01 — Introduction
📘

What is Globalisation?

Globalisation is the process through which countries become more connected through trade, technology, communication, and culture. It allows goods, services, information, and ideas to move easily across borders.

Why it matters:

  • It affects economies worldwide
  • It influences culture and traditions
  • It changes how people communicate and work
02 — History
🗺️

How Globalisation Started

🐪
Silk Road Era — Trade routes like the Silk Road connected Asia and Europe centuries ago, enabling exchange of goods and culture.
⚙️
Industrial Revolution — Mass production and steam engines dramatically increased international trade and economic links.
🌐
Modern Technology & Internet — Digital communication and the internet accelerated globalisation to an unprecedented pace.
03 — Economy
🚢

Globalisation & International Trade

Countries exchange products and services across the world. Trade allows countries to specialise and grow economically.

🏭
Exports by Country
  • China manufactures electronics
  • Middle East exports oil
  • Europe produces cars
  • Pakistan exports textiles
📦
How Trade Works
  • Nations export and import goods creating deep economic interdependence
  • Manufacturing spans multiple countries — one product, many borders
  • Small businesses reach global customers online
04 — Technology
💻

Technology Connecting the World

Modern technology has made globalisation faster than ever before.

  • The internet connects billions of people across every continent instantly
  • Social media spreads information and news around the world within seconds
  • Online meetings allow seamless international collaboration daily
  • Businesses operate on a truly global scale across borders

05 — Culture
🎬

Globalisation & Cultural Exchange

Globalisation spreads culture across the world, creating rich blends of traditions.

  • Korean music (K-Pop) and movies are loved worldwide
  • American movies are watched in every corner of the globe
  • International food is available in cities of every country
  • Fashion trends spread online within hours

07 — Advantages
📈

The Benefits of a Connected World

Globalisation has improved living standards in many countries.

💰
Economic Growth
  • Drives innovation and entrepreneurship
  • Creates millions of jobs across borders
🤝
Global Cooperation
  • Better access to global technology & ideas
  • Nations tackle shared problems together
🌐
Access to Technology
  • Developing nations access advanced tech
  • Digital tools reach remote areas
💼
Global Job Opportunities
  • Remote work opens international roles
  • Skilled workers find jobs worldwide
08 — Challenges
⚠️

The Challenges We Must Face

🏪
Economic Pressure
  • Local businesses struggle against global companies
  • Economic inequality between rich and poor nations grows
🎭
Cultural Identity
  • Risk of losing unique local traditions and heritage
  • Dominant cultures overshadow smaller ones
🌿
Environment
  • Environmental issues due to industrial growth
  • Carbon emissions from global shipping
🧑‍💼
Job Displacement
  • Automation replaces traditional jobs
  • Factory jobs move to cheaper countries
09 — Pros vs Cons
⚖️

Globalisation: Pros & Cons

✅
Pros
  • Economic development & growth
  • Innovation and new technologies
  • Easier global communication
  • Cultural exchange and diversity
❌
Cons
  • Cultural loss and homogenisation
  • Job displacement in some sectors
  • Economic imbalance between nations
  • Environmental degradation

Countries must balance these effects through smart policy and international cooperation.

12 — Our Opinion
💡

Our Perspective

"Globalisation has woven the world closer together and unlocked remarkable opportunities — yet nations must act with wisdom to protect their cultures and build economic systems that are truly fair for all."

We believe countries should:

  • Protect and celebrate their culture and heritage
  • Support local industries alongside global trade
  • Encourage genuine international cooperation

Balanced globalisation can truly improve the world.
